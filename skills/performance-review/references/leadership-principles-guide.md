# Leadership Principles Guide (For Reviewing Managers)

This reference is used when writing peer reviews for people leaders/managers. Leadership Principles are management requirements for leaders at ByteDance - the bigger the management scope and impact, the more important these are.

---

## Rating Scale for Leadership Principles

| Rating | Description | Key Criteria |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **E** | Highly recognizes Leadership Principles; consistently follows all and sets example for team; drives positive influence across team or company; role model | Result: Team continuously achieves great results + exceptional culture. Extensiveness: Exceptional in every principle. Continuity: Demonstrates consistently |
| **M+** | Consistently practices Leadership Principles with excellent performance in some; team demonstrates solid outputs and great culture | Similar to E but outcomes and extensiveness are less significant |
| **M** | Meets requirements on most occasions; team consistently produces work outputs and adequate culture | Occasional lapses but no significant negative impact |
| **M-** | Sometimes fails to meet requirements; needs improvement in some principles; team falls short of expectations | Issues cause negative impact on team growth, culture, outcome |
| **I** | Often demonstrates behaviors that breach Leadership Principles; causes negative influence or serious harm | Severe impact, multiple repeated issues, unable to correct. Crosses bottom line (unfair, dishonest, bad cultural example) |

## The 4 Sections of Leadership Principles

### Section 1: Have Sound Judgment in Complex Situations (判断力)

| Principle | Is (M+ and E) | Is Not (M- and I) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Be mission and vision driven** | Resonates with business value, strong sense of purpose, communicates mission to inspire team; considers 3-5 year timeframe; in-depth business understanding | Unclear goals, short-term focus, content with status quo; lacks medium/long-term direction |
| **Understand the fundamentals to develop sound judgment** | Identifies key issues, prioritizes, utilizes resources efficiently; helps team analyze complex problems; makes decisions at critical moments | Misjudges key issues, misallocates resources; unable to prioritize; ambiguous decisions without professional guidance |
| **Focus on actual impact** | Communicates with front-line colleagues, studies specific cases, experiences products firsthand, masters firsthand information | Knows little about frontline work; relies on processed second-hand data; lacks attention to detail |

### Section 2: Create a Collaborative Environment (协作环境)

| Principle | Is (M+ and E) | Is Not (M- and I) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Small ego, big picture** | Thinks from leader's and skip-level leader's perspectives; takes ownership where unclear; motivates team ownership | Views only from own team perspective; avoids responsibility; waits for things to resolve |
| **Leverage collective wisdom** | Listens to others' opinions, actively seeks different voices, can still make final call | Authoritarian decision-making; rejects different viewpoints; OR overly democratic, shies away from decisions |
| **Lead by example** | Practices ByteStyle; demonstrates integrity, openness, pragmatism; high standards; says no to 'Managing Up'; hands-on | Does not practice ByteStyle; team lacks trust and competitiveness |

### Section 3: Develop High-Performing Teams (团队建设)

| Principle | Is (M+ and E) | Is Not (M- and I) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Lead people based on their qualities** | Identifies fundamental requirements, recruits above-average capabilities; introduces colleagues with diverse backgrounds; builds diverse talent pool | Relies excessively on past experience; low hiring standards; backgrounds/experience not diverse enough |
| **Foster the growth of team members** | Provides space, delegates power, offers counsel and motivation; trains core members; ensures rapid growth; healthy talent distribution; builds succession planning | Lacks recognition/delegation; doesn't motivate great talent; team growth is slow; no apparent successor |
| **Manage with firm principles** | Fearless and patient with difficult management problems; gives differentiated rewards/incentives; keeps team lean; continuously improves performance/culture | Lacks sound judgment on keeping team competitive; fails to give differentiated ratings; overstaffed; unhealthy talent flow |

### Section 4: Embrace Challenges and Achieve Results (拥抱挑战)

| Principle | Is (M+ and E) | Is Not (M- and I) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Set ambitious and clear goals** | Clear goal in mind; strong mechanisms to manage and communicate | Doesn't have clear goal; lacks sound goal management/communication |
| **Be resilient and achieve results** | Achieves mission in face of difficulties; resilient; faces problems head-on; strong drive to achieve long-term output | Fearful of challenges; lacks resilience; unwilling to deal with difficult situations; can't get desired results |

## How to Use in Peer Review

When reviewing a manager/people leader, evaluate against the 4 sections:

### Structure for Manager Peer Review

**Output Section:**
- **Accomplishments**: Assess their leadership behaviors using the 4 sections
- **Areas of Improvement**: Identify which leadership principles need strengthening

**ByteStyle Section:**
- Leadership Principles supplement ByteStyle (they're not the same)
- Managers are evaluated on BOTH ByteStyle AND Leadership Principles

### Mapping Observations to Leadership Principles

| Observation | Maps to Principle |
|-------------|------------------|
| Good prioritization, resource allocation | Understand the fundamentals |
| Inspires team with vision | Be mission and vision driven |
| Gets firsthand information, talks to frontline | Focus on actual impact |
| Takes ownership beyond their scope | Small ego, big picture |
| Listens to different viewpoints before deciding | Leverage collective wisdom |
| Practices what they preach | Lead by example |
| Hires for capability, not just credentials | Lead people based on their qualities |
| Gives feedback, delegates, mentors | Foster growth of team members |
| Manages low performers, rewards high performers | Manage with firm principles |
| Clear goals, good communication | Set ambitious and clear goals |
| Pushes through despite difficulties | Be resilient and achieve results |

### Example Feedback (Areas of Improvement)

**Section 1 - Sound Judgment:**
```
Could improve in "understanding the fundamentals to develop sound judgement." In [specific project], the team struggled with prioritization, and resources were allocated to lower-impact work. Earlier identification of key issues would have helped the team focus on critical deliverables.
```

**Section 3 - Build Great Teams:**
```
Needs improvement in "foster the growth of team members." Team members reported receiving limited feedback and few enriching work opportunities for growth. More regular 1:1s with developmental focus would help team members understand their growth path.
```

## Red Flags (M- or I indicators)

**Judgment Issues:**
- Unable to prioritize, team wastes resources
- Makes ambiguous decisions without professional guidance
- Relies on second-hand data, doesn't know frontline reality

**Collaboration Issues:**
- Only views things from own team's perspective
- Authoritarian decision-making, rejects different viewpoints
- Creates environment of "managing up"

**Team Building Issues:**
- Doesn't manage low performers
- No differentiated rewards based on performance
- Overstaffed team, unhealthy talent flow
- No succession planning

**Results Issues:**
- No clear goals or goal communication
- Fearful of challenges, avoids difficult situations
- Unable to achieve long-term output

## Typical Cases by Rating

### M+ Typical Patterns
- Setting long-term vision, addressing team concerns about short-term tactics, assigning enriching growth opportunities
- Navigating org changes with big-picture mindset, ensuring handoffs don't drop deliverables
- Focusing on HR data (organizational efficiency, talent density, talent mobility) with measurable goals and weekly reviews

### M Typical Patterns
- Manages low performers with firm principles, rallies team during transitions
- BUT: some delays in identifying severity of issues, could have acted sooner
- Makes sound judgments in complex situations, opposes legacy decisions when needed for user impact
- BUT: could better align OKRs with team and connect daily work to mission/vision

### M- Typical Patterns
- Lacks in-depth thinking; goes for superficial fixes instead of root cause analysis
- Long list of priorities but most are not critical; lacks judgment on what has impact
- Prioritizes style over substance (docs/slides over outcomes)
- Unable to make decisions independently; easily swayed without solid reasoning
- Doesn't prioritize well nor direct resources to most important goals
- Tells team to work 80+ hours instead of setting up for success with proper resourcing
- Overly specialized divisions; complex collaboration processes; unclear outputs
- Lacks diversity/inclusivity in global team settings
- Little investment in fostering team member growth; short/poorly-executed review conversations
- Scope overlaps and unclear division of work creating inefficiency

### I Typical Patterns
- Avoids managing underperformers across multiple review cycles
- Creates environment where team is afraid to give honest feedback
- Vague division of work with conflicts and overlapping workstreams
- Not present for team, no guidance or support
- Distorts data, takes shortcuts, blames others when things go wrong
- Demands strict compliance without questioning

## Writing Leadership Principles Feedback

### For Accomplishments:
Map observed positive behaviors to the 4 sections. Use specific examples:
```
[Section]: [Leader] demonstrated [principle] by [specific action]. This resulted in [outcome for team/business].
```

### For Areas of Improvement:
Identify which section(s) need strengthening with specific evidence:
```
[Section] - [Principle]: [Specific observation of gap]. [Concrete example]. [Suggested improvement].
```

### Key Differences from ByteStyle:
- ByteStyle = personal work style (applies to all)
- Leadership Principles = management behaviors (applies to people leaders only)
- A manager is evaluated on BOTH
- Leadership Principles focus on TEAM OUTCOMES, not just personal behaviors

---

## Bottom Line Violations (I rating)

These behaviors breach the bottom line:
- Acting unfairly (favoritism, bias)
- Acting dishonestly (distorting data, hiding problems)
- Setting a bad cultural example
- Preventing team from giving honest feedback
- Repeated issues with no improvement after feedback
