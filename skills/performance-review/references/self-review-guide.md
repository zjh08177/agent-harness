# Self-Review Writing Guide

## ⚠️ TOP PRIORITY: Manager's 3-1 Framework

**Before anything else, read `references/manager-3-1-framework.md`**

This contains direct feedback from the manager on what matters most for 3-1 evaluation. The two critical points:

1. **SEPARATE direct impact from indirect impact** - Don't mix them. Direct impact must stand out.
2. **"Your Diff" is THE CORE 3-1 judgment point** - What's the difference with you vs. without you?

---

## Core Principle

Self-Review evaluates **"how well you did"** based on the comparison of actual outputs vs. reasonable expectations. It's NOT a repeat of Key Outputs.

## Prerequisites

**Before writing Self-Review, read these files in order:**
1. **`references/manager-3-1-framework.md`** - TOP PRIORITY: Manager's 5-dimension evaluation framework
2. `references/2025-key-outputs.md` - Ground accomplishments in specific Key Outputs
3. `references/calibration-rubric-3-1.md` - Align accomplishments with 3-1 level competency criteria

The Self-Review must demonstrate competency at the target level by:
- **Clearly separating direct vs indirect impact**
- **Articulating your personal differentiation ("Your Diff")**
- Connecting accomplishments to calibration criteria

## ⭐ The 5 Evaluation Dimensions (Manager Framework)

| # | Dimension | Key Question to Answer |
|---|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Product Ownership & Outcome** | What are you DIRECTLY responsible for and accountable for? |
| 2 | **Your Diff** ⭐ MOST CRITICAL | What's different with you vs without you? |
| 3 | **Complexity & Technical Ownership** | Did you make medium/long-term plans? Handle ambiguity? |
| 4 | **Direction & Quality** | Was the direction correct? Solution mature? Quality sustainable? |
| 5 | **Team & Org Impact** | Did you drive team capability, not just personal achievement? |

### Writing Rules from Manager Feedback

**Rule 1: SEPARATE Direct vs Indirect Impact**

DO:
```
[DIRECT - I was accountable]
As the tech owner for AIGC offline processing, I made the decision to use X protocol and drove the cross-team integration.

[INDIRECT - I contributed]
The 13x publish rate improvement was a team effort. My direct contribution was the pipeline architecture enabling 70% of new effect production.
```

DON'T:
```
Led integration that resulted in 13x improvement.
(Mixes direct action with indirect metric - unclear what YOU caused)
```

**Rule 2: HIGHLIGHT Your Personal Differentiation**

For every accomplishment, answer:
- What would have happened without me?
- What critical decision did I make that others wouldn't have?
- What did I push for that changed the outcome?

**Rule 3: Show Technical Judgment, Not Just Execution**

Weak: "Delivered workflow manager for 7 projects."
Strong: "Advocated for protocol-first design over implementation-first. This required pushing back on timeline pressure, but resulted in 75% dev time reduction."

## Calibration-Aligned Writing Strategy

### The Three Dimensions (3-1 Level)

| Dimension | Key Criteria | What Calibrators Look For |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------------|
| **Technical Competencies** | Architecture understanding, long-term planning, platform expertise, applying advanced experience | Evidence of strategic technical thinking, not just execution |
| **Business Competencies** | Product growth awareness, resource management, team leadership, accountability | Ownership of outcomes, not just tasks |
| **Team Contributions** | Echelon building, collective skills enhancement, cross-functional sharing | Impact on others' growth, not just personal achievement |

### Writing Template (Calibration-Aligned)

```
[Accomplishment from Key Output] demonstrates [specific 3-1 criteria from calibration-rubric-3-1.md] by [concrete evidence/metrics].
```

**Example:**
```
Leading the AIGC Arch Tiger team to define the Universal AIGC Workflow Protocol demonstrates "clearly understanding the app's complete technical architecture and making technical strategies for the medium and long term" (Technical #1). The protocol now serves as the foundation for both client and server workflow managers, adopted across 7 major projects.
```

## Structure

### Accomplishments (做得好的)

**Template:**
```
In [work/project from Key Outputs], based on [needs/project objectives], by [actions taken / difficulties overcome], achieved [outputs and effects that exceed expectations]. This demonstrates [3-1 calibration criteria].
```

**Key elements:**
- **Must reference specific Key Outputs** - Link each accomplishment to a numbered deliverable
- **Must map to calibration criteria** - Explicitly state which 3-1 competency is demonstrated
- Compare against expectations (industry benchmarks, targets, peer performance)
- Include specific examples and data from Key Outputs

**Mapping Key Outputs to Calibration Criteria:**

| Key Output | Technical Competencies | Business Competencies | Team Contributions |
|------------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **KO1: Effect & AIGC Offline Processing** | #1 Architecture strategy, #4 Advanced experience | #1 Product growth, #2 Resource management | |
| **KO2: AIGC Arch Foundation** | #1 Architecture, #2 Broad view, #4 Advanced experience | #1 Business growth, #2 Planning, #3 Team leadership | #2 Technical standards |
| **KO3: Team Leadership** | | | #1 Echelon building, #2 Collective skills, #3 Cross-functional sharing |

### Areas of Improvement (待改进的)

**Template:**
```
In [work/project], [goals not achieved / capabilities needing improvement], causing [problems/impact]. To strengthen [specific 3-1 calibration criteria], I plan to [improvement action].
```

**Key elements:**
- Be specific and honest—generic statements are unhelpful
- Identify which 3-1 criteria need strengthening
- Include forward-looking improvement plans aligned to calibration gaps

## Good Examples

### Accomplishments (Calibration-Aligned)

**Technical Competency - KO1:**
```
In the AIGC offline processing pipeline delivery, I led cross-team integration with Server, TTEH, AME, and Camera teams to launch TikTok's first i2v feature in March. This demonstrates "clearly understanding the app's complete technical architecture and making technical strategies for the medium and long term" (Technical #1). By establishing clear API contracts and proactive communication, we achieved 100 QPS capacity at launch with zero P0 incidents. The pipeline now supports 70% of new effect production, contributing to the 13x improvement in AI effect publish rate (0.04% → 0.53%).
```

**Business Competency - KO2:**
```
As tech lead for the AIGC Arch Tiger team, I defined the Universal AIGC Workflow Protocol that became the foundation for both client and server workflow managers. This demonstrates "engaging in product planning, managing resources appropriately, and assuming accountability for product results" (Business #2). By advocating for protocol-first design, I drove adoption across 7 major projects in Creation and Social verticals, reducing integration time from 8 days to 2 days per project—a 75% efficiency gain.
```

**Team Contribution - KO3:**
```
Through regular 1:1s and career path discussions, I helped team members align personal growth with project goals. This demonstrates "overseeing echelon building and fostering individual growth within the team" (Team #1). By introducing small MR practices and AI-assisted code review, I also enhanced "collective technical skills of the team by setting technical standards" (Team #2), resulting in fewer review cycles and faster merge times.
```

### Areas of Improvement (Calibration-Aligned)

**Business Competency Gap:**
```
In the AI Theater integration, I could have started architectural discussions earlier. The late alignment on ID recording and management caused rework, delaying the initial timeline by two weeks. To strengthen "engaging in product planning and assuming accountability for product results" (Business #2), I will initiate architecture alignment meetings at project kickoff.
```

**Technical Competency Gap:**
```
While I have deep iOS expertise, my understanding of server-side architecture could be stronger. To better demonstrate "foundational skills of working across platforms" (3-1 Keyword), I plan to participate in backend design reviews and shadow server engineers on cross-platform projects.
```

## ByteStyle Dimension

Reflects working styles and methods. Key behaviors to highlight:
- **Always Day 1**: Entrepreneurial mindset, continuous improvement
- **Be Candid and Clear**: Direct communication, transparency
- **Be Grounded and Dedicated**: Execution focus, attention to detail
- **Be Open and Humble**: Learning mindset, accepting feedback
- **Aim for the Highest**: High standards, excellence pursuit
- **Seek Truth and Be Pragmatic**: Data-driven, fact-based decisions

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| **Mixing direct & indirect impact** | SEPARATE them - direct impact must stand out |
| Not showing "Your Diff" | Explicitly state what's different with you vs without you |
| Showing execution only, no judgment | Show technical decisions, trade-offs, direction choices |
| Repeat Key Outputs verbatim | Evaluate HOW WELL you did, not WHAT you did |
| Generic accomplishments without calibration link | Explicitly map to 3-1 criteria |
| All accomplishments, no improvements | Show self-awareness with genuine improvement areas |
| Generic improvement like "communicate better" | Specific: "To strengthen Business #2, I will initiate early alignment meetings" |
| Focus only on Technical dimension | Cover all 3 dimensions (Technical, Business, Team) |

### Manager's Specific Feedback on Current Key Outputs

> "直接影响和间接影响的产出混在一起了。造成直接影响的内容不突出。需要在自评里增加描述。突出个人的差异化影响。"

**What this means:**
- Don't just say "delivered X which led to Y metric"
- Clearly state: "I was directly accountable for X. I made decision Z. The Y metric was a broader team effort, but my specific contribution was [...]"
