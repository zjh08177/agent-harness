# Prior Art Search Workflow

## What is Prior Art?

Prior art is the body of knowledge existing before your invention, including:
- Published US and international patent applications
- Granted patents
- Academic papers and journals
- Books and publications
- Websites and social media
- Product documentation
- Conference presentations

## Why Prior Art Search Matters

- **Patentability**: Determines if invention is "new" and "non-obvious"
- **Claim Strategy**: Helps craft claims that avoid existing art
- **Risk Assessment**: Identifies potential blocking patents
- **Invalidation Risk**: PTAB invalidation rate reached 71% in early 2024

## Search Strategy Components

### 1. Text/Keyword Searching
Search for terms describing your invention concept:
- Core technical terms
- Synonyms and variations
- Industry-specific terminology
- Both English and Chinese terms for international coverage

### 2. Classification Searching
Use Cooperative Patent Classification (CPC) codes:
- Identify relevant classification codes for your technology area
- Search within those classifications for related patents
- CPC codes used by both USPTO and EPO

### 3. Citation Searching
Examine patents that:
- Are cited BY relevant patents
- CITE relevant patents
- Share inventors or assignees with relevant patents

## Search Process for AIGC/Workflow Patents

### Step 1: Define Search Scope

Identify key concepts in your invention:
- **Technical domain**: AIGC, generative AI, workflow orchestration
- **Specific features**: Cross-platform, protocol-based, workflow engine
- **Implementation details**: Client-server, SDK, API design

### Step 2: Generate Keyword Lists

**Primary Keywords:**
- AIGC workflow
- Generative AI pipeline
- Cross-platform workflow engine
- Workflow protocol
- AI content generation orchestration

**Secondary Keywords:**
- Workflow automation
- AI model orchestration
- Content generation pipeline
- Multi-platform AI integration
- Workflow definition language

**Technical Keywords:**
- Workflow graph execution
- Task dependency resolution
- Cross-platform SDK
- Protocol buffer workflow
- Distributed AI execution

### Step 3: Construct Search Queries

**Google Patents Examples:**
```
"workflow engine" AND ("generative AI" OR "AIGC")
"cross-platform" AND "workflow protocol" AND "content generation"
"AI workflow" AND "orchestration" AND "mobile"
```

**USPTO Search Examples:**
```
ABST/(workflow AND (AI OR artificial intelligence) AND protocol)
ACLM/(cross-platform AND workflow AND engine)
```

### Step 4: Review and Analyze Results

For each relevant patent found:
1. **Read claims**: Focus on independent claims
2. **Review figures**: Understand system architecture
3. **Check priority date**: Determine if it's true prior art
4. **Assess similarity**: How close is it to your invention?
5. **Note differences**: Document what makes your invention distinct

### Step 5: Document Findings

Create a prior art summary table:

| Patent/Publication | Title | Key Claims | Relevance | Differentiation |
|-------------------|-------|------------|-----------|-----------------|
| US12345678 | ... | ... | High/Med/Low | How yours differs |

## Documenting Novelty

After prior art search, document:

1. **What exists**: Summary of closest prior art
2. **What's missing**: Gaps in existing solutions
3. **Your contribution**: How your invention fills those gaps
4. **Technical advantages**: Measurable improvements over prior art

## Tips for Effective Searching

1. **Cast wide initially**: Start broad, then narrow
2. **Use multiple databases**: Don't rely on just one source
3. **Search internationally**: Only 19% of patents filed in US
4. **Include non-patent literature**: Academic papers, publications
5. **Document everything**: Keep records of searches performed
6. **Iterate**: Refine searches based on initial findings
7. **Check recent filings**: Published applications not yet granted
